# Correlation Breakdown Early Warning System

A market risk management project that proposes an early warning framework to detect **correlation breakdowns** before they significantly impact portfolio performance.

This project was completed as part of my internship at **ZeTheta Algorithms Private Limited**.

---

## Project Overview

Portfolio diversification assumes that different asset classes behave differently under varying market conditions. However, during periods of financial stress, asset correlations often increase sharply, causing diversification benefits to diminish.

This project proposes a practical **Early Warning System** that monitors market indicators to detect rising correlation risk and generate timely alerts for portfolio managers.

---

## Problem Statement

Traditional portfolio risk models often rely on historical correlations, assuming that relationships between assets remain stable over time.

In reality, market crises such as the **2008 Global Financial Crisis**, **COVID-19 Pandemic**, and **IL&FS Crisis** demonstrate that correlations can change rapidly, exposing portfolios to higher-than-expected risk.

---

## Objectives

- Understand asset correlation and diversification.
- Study correlation breakdown during market stress.
- Design an early warning framework.
- Monitor rolling correlations.
- Integrate volatility and credit spread indicators.
- Generate proactive portfolio risk alerts.

---

## Early Warning Framework

The proposed framework follows six stages:

```
Market Data
      ↓
Rolling Correlation
      ↓
Volatility Analysis
      ↓
Credit Spread Analysis
      ↓
Risk Score Calculation
      ↓
Alert Generation
```
